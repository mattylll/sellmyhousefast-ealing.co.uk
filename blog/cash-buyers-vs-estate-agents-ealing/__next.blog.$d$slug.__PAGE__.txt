This includes two to four weeks for marketing preparation, four to twelve weeks to find and agree with a buyer, and eight to twelve weeks for conveyancing. In premium areas like Ealing Broadway, where properties above £700,000 face a smaller buyer pool, marketing periods can extend beyond six months. Even in busier areas like Acton, where demand is strong, the full process rarely takes less than four months.</p><h3>Cash Buyer Route</h3><p>A reputable cash buyer can complete the purchase in as little as seven days, with a typical timeline of two to three weeks. There is no marketing period, no viewings, and no chain. From the moment you accept the offer, the process moves in a single, predictable stream towards a guaranteed completion date.</p><h2>Financial Comparison: The Real Numbers</h2><p>Let us work through the numbers for a typical Ealing property valued at £560,000.</p><h3>Estate Agent Sale</h3><ul><li><strong>Sale price:</strong> £560,000 (assuming full asking price achieved)</li><li><strong>Estate agent fees (1.5% + VAT):</strong> -£10,080</li><li><strong>Solicitor fees:</strong> -£1,500</li><li><strong>Holding costs over 6 months:</strong> -£12,000 to -£18,000 (mortgage payments, council tax, insurance, maintenance)</li><li><strong>Estimated net proceeds:</strong> £530,420 to £536,420</li></ul><p>And there is a roughly 30% chance the sale collapses, adding further months of holding costs and requiring a fresh start.</p><h3>Cash Buyer Sale</h3><ul><li><strong>Sale price (88% of market value):</strong> £492,800</li><li><strong>Estate agent fees:</strong> £0</li><li><strong>Solicitor fees:</strong> £0 (covered by buyer)</li><li><strong>Holding costs (2-3 weeks):</strong> negligible</li><li><strong>Estimated net proceeds:</strong> £492,800</li></ul><p>The difference in net proceeds is approximately £37,600 to £43,600 — significant, but substantially less than the headline price gap suggests. And the cash sale comes with a guarantee of completion that the traditional route simply cannot match.</p><h2>Risk Assessment</h2><h3>Chain Collapse</h3><p>In Ealing's market, chain risk is particularly acute. Higher property values mean larger mortgages, more complex chains, and greater vulnerability to buyer financing issues. The borough's mix of first-time buyers stretching their finances and upsizers with multiple dependencies creates chains that are inherently fragile. When a chain collapses — which happens to roughly one in three transactions — you lose months of time and thousands in costs.</p><p>With a cash buyer, there is no chain at all. The risk of collapse is effectively zero.</p><h3>Gazundering</h3><p>In a cooling or uncertain market, buyers may reduce their offers just before exchange — a practice known as gazundering. The higher the property value, the more tempting this tactic becomes for buyers who know the seller has invested months in the process and may feel pressured to accept a lower figure rather than start again.</p><p>Cash buyers do not gazunder. The offer agreed is the offer paid. This certainty has real financial value, even though it does not appear on a simple price comparison spreadsheet.</p><h3>Market Movement</h3><p>During the six months a traditional sale takes, the market can shift. If prices soften — as they sometimes do in response to interest rate changes, economic events, or seasonal patterns — you may end up selling for less than the original offer you accepted, after the buyer renegotiates based on a lower lender valuation. A cash sale locks in the price and completes before market movements can affect the outcome.</p><h2>The Experience Factor</h2><h3>Traditional Sale Experience</h3><p>Selling through an agent means weeks of keeping your property show-ready, accommodating viewings (often at inconvenient times), dealing with feedback from buyers who were never serious, negotiating offers and counter-offers, and then enduring months of anxiety as the chain progresses — or stalls. For families in Ealing with children, professionals working from home, or anyone dealing with difficult personal circumstances, this process is genuinely exhausting.</p><h3>Cash Sale Experience</h3><p>The cash sale process is minimal in its demands. After your initial enquiry and a property visit of around 30 minutes, the process moves into the hands of solicitors. There are no viewings, no staging, no open houses, and no negotiations with amateur property bargain-hunters. Your involvement is limited to reviewing and signing legal documents. The emotional and practical burden is dramatically lighter.</p><h2>When Each Option Makes Sense</h2><h3>Choose an Estate Agent When:</h3><ul><li>You have no time pressure and can comfortably wait six months or more</li><li>Your property is in pristine condition in a high-demand area like Ealing Broadway or Pitshanger Lane</li><li>The price difference between market value and a cash offer is significant enough to outweigh the costs and risks</li><li>You are confident that your likely buyer will not have chain complications</li><li>You are emotionally prepared for the process of viewings, negotiations, and uncertainty</li></ul><h3>Choose a Cash Buyer When:</h3><ul><li>You need to sell within days or weeks, not months</li><li>You are dealing with divorce, bereavement, financial difficulty, or relocation</li><li>Your property needs work that traditional buyers would be deterred by</li><li>You have already experienced a failed sale through an agent</li><li>The property is tenanted and you want to avoid gaining vacant possession</li><li>You want to become a chain-free buyer for your next property purchase</li><li>The certainty and simplicity of a guaranteed sale appeals to you more than the possibility of a marginally higher price</li></ul><h2>A Hybrid Approach</h2><p>Some Ealing homeowners adopt a hybrid strategy: they list with an estate agent but set a time limit. If the property has not sold within a defined period — say, eight weeks — they switch to a cash buyer. This approach gives the open market a chance but provides a backstop that prevents the process from dragging on indefinitely.</p><p>If you are considering this approach, it is worth getting a cash buyer valuation upfront.
