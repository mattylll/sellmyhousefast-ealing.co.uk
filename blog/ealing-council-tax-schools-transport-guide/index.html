Ealing Council Tax, Schools & Transport Guide for Sellers

By Sell My House Fast Ealing

Local Factors That Influence Your Ealing Property's Value and Appeal

When buyers evaluate a property in the London Borough of Ealing, they look beyond the bricks and mortar. Council tax costs, school access, and transport connectivity all play a significant role in their decision-making — and in what they are willing to pay. Understanding how these factors affect your property's appeal can help you position your sale effectively and set realistic price expectations.

This guide provides detailed information on the key local considerations that shape the property market across Ealing's neighbourhoods.

Council Tax in Ealing

How Ealing Compares

Council tax in the London Borough of Ealing sits in the mid-range for Greater London boroughs. The current Band D rate is approximately £1,900, including the Greater London Authority precept. This is comparable to neighbouring boroughs like Hounslow and Brent, and lower than some outer London boroughs that face greater financial pressures.

The council provides a wide range of services funded by council tax, including waste collection, road maintenance, parks, libraries, and social care. The borough's financial position has been relatively stable compared to some London councils, which can provide reassurance to prospective buyers concerned about potential future increases.

Council Tax Bands Across the Borough

Council tax bands are based on estimated property values as at April 1991, and the distribution varies considerably across Ealing's neighbourhoods:

  • Ealing Broadway and Pitshanger: Higher concentration of Band E, F, and G properties, reflecting the larger, more valuable housing stock. Band F annual charges are approximately £2,700; Band G approximately £3,170.
  • Acton: Mixed bands, with newer developments and converted flats in lower bands and period houses in Bands D to F.
  • Hanwell: Predominantly Band C and D, with annual charges ranging from approximately £1,690 to £1,900.
  • Southall: Mix of bands, with terraced housing stock typically in Bands B to D.
  • Greenford and Perivale: Predominantly Band C and D, reflecting the inter-war semi-detached housing stock.
  • Northolt: Higher proportion of Band A and B properties, with annual charges from approximately £1,270 to £1,480.

Council tax band information is relevant to buyers because it affects their ongoing cost of ownership. In lower-value areas like Northolt, where Band A or B charges are modest, this is a selling point. In premium areas, higher bands are accepted as part of the overall cost of living in a desirable location.

Empty Property Council Tax

If you are selling a vacant property — perhaps inherited or being sold as part of a landlord exit — full council tax is payable on empty properties in Ealing. Some exemptions apply for probate situations, but these are time-limited. Long-term empty properties may attract a premium. This is a practical reason to pursue a fast sale and minimise the period of vacancy.

Schools in Ealing

The School Premium

School catchment areas are among the most powerful drivers of family home prices in the London Borough of Ealing. Properties within the catchment of highly rated schools consistently command premiums of 5% to 15% over similar homes in less desirable catchments. For sellers of family-sized properties, school information should be a central element of any marketing strategy.

Primary Schools

Ealing has a large number of primary schools, including community schools, church schools, and academies. Several have achieved Outstanding ratings from Ofsted, and these tend to have the most dramatic impact on local property demand. Schools in Ealing Broadway, Pitshanger, and Hanwell are particularly sought-after by families, with small catchment areas that intensify competition for places.

Notable primary schools that influence property demand include those in the North Ealing, Montpelier, and Hanwell areas. If your property falls within the catchment of a well-regarded primary school, this is a genuine asset that can command a price premium and should be highlighted in all marketing materials.

Secondary Schools

Ealing's secondary school landscape includes comprehensive schools, academies, and grammar schools. The borough is home to several high-performing secondary schools that attract families from across West London and beyond. The selective schools, which admit pupils based on examination, are a particular draw for families in the borough, and proximity to these schools can influence property demand across a wide area.

The overall standard of secondary education in Ealing has improved markedly in recent years, with several schools achieving significantly improved results. However, performance varies, and buyers will research specific schools carefully. Being able to provide accurate information about nearby schools — including Ofsted ratings, distance, and recent results — can help your sale.

Impact on Different Areas

The school premium is most pronounced in Ealing Broadway, Pitshanger, Hanwell, and parts of Acton — areas where families with school-age children are the primary buyer demographic. In areas like Northolt and Greenford, where the buyer mix includes more first-time buyers and investors, the school effect is present but less dominant.

Transport Links

The Elizabeth Line (Crossrail)

The Elizabeth line is the single most significant transport asset in the London Borough of Ealing, serving five stations within the borough:

  • Ealing Broadway: Also served by District and Central lines. Elizabeth line journey to Paddington: 10 minutes; to Liverpool Street: 28 minutes; to Canary Wharf: 35 minutes.
  • West Ealing: Elizabeth line direct services. Journey to Paddington: 12 minutes.
  • Acton Main Line: Elizabeth line services. Journey to Paddington: 8 minutes.
  • Hanwell: Elizabeth line and GWR services. Journey to Paddington: 15 minutes.
  • Southall: Elizabeth line and GWR services. Journey to Paddington: 17 minutes.

The Elizabeth line's impact on property values cannot be overstated. It has made Ealing one of the best-connected outer London boroughs for commuters heading to the West End, the City, Docklands, and Heathrow Airport (via interchange at Paddington). Properties within walking distance of Elizabeth line stations attract a measurable premium.

London Underground

Beyond the Elizabeth line, Ealing benefits from excellent Underground services:

  • Central line: Ealing Broadway, West Acton, North Acton, East Acton, Perivale, Greenford, Northolt
  • District line: Ealing Broadway, Ealing Common, Acton Town, South Ealing
  • Piccadilly line: Acton Town, Ealing Common, South Ealing, Northfields

This density of tube stations means that almost every part of the borough has convenient Underground access, supporting property values even in areas not directly served by the Elizabeth line.

Bus Network

TfL operates an extensive bus network across Ealing, with key routes running along Uxbridge Road (the borough's main east-west corridor), connecting all major centres from Acton through Ealing Broadway to Southall and beyond. Night buses serve several routes, adding to the area's accessibility.

Road Access

The A40 Western Avenue provides direct road access to Central London and the M40 motorway heading towards Oxford and Birmingham. The A4 Great West Road serves the southern part of the borough. Proximity to Heathrow Airport — approximately 20 to 30 minutes by road from most parts of Ealing — is an additional selling point, particularly for international buyers and professionals who travel frequently.
